# McKinsey — Business Analysis

## Mood

Professional, authoritative, data-driven. Clean layouts with generous whitespace.
Structured for clarity — every slide communicates one key insight. Alternates
between white and dark-blue backgrounds to create visual rhythm and emphasis.
Minimal decoration; let data and text speak.

## Colors

- Primary: `#003A70` — deep McKinsey navy, used for dark-background slides, titles on white slides
- Secondary: `#0078D4` — vivid blue, used sparingly to highlight key data or insights
- Accent: `#00A3E0` — lighter accent blue for charts, callouts, supporting visuals
- Warm Accent: `#D4A843` — muted gold, occasional emphasis (awards, key metrics)
- Background Light: `#FFFFFF` — white, primary slide background
- Background Dark: `#003A70` — navy, alternate slide background for section breaks and emphasis
- Alt Surface: `#F2F4F7` — very light gray, for content cards or subtle separation
- Text Primary: `#1A1A2E` — near-black on light backgrounds
- Text on Dark: `#FFFFFF` — white text on dark navy backgrounds
- Text Secondary: `#5A6A7A` — muted gray for captions, footnotes
- Divider: `#C8D1DC` — light gray for horizontal rules and separators

## Typography

- h1: 40px, bold (action title — the slide's key message)
- h2: 28px, bold (section headers, chart titles)
- h3: 22px, semibold (sub-headers)
- Body (p): 16px, regular
- Caption: 13px, regular, Text Secondary color

## Layout Principles

- **One message per slide**: the h1 is a complete sentence stating the insight.
- **Action titles**: h1 is not a label ("Revenue") but a takeaway ("Revenue grew 23% YoY driven by APAC expansion").
- White slides for content; dark navy slides for section transitions and key conclusions.
- Prefer left-aligned text. Center alignment only for section-transition slides.
- Charts and data visuals should be clean — no gridlines, no 3D effects, minimal labels.
